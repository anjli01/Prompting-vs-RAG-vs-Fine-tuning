WHEN AN AI MODEL GIVES YOU A BAD ANSWER, THERE ARE ACTUALLY THREE VERY DIFFERENT REASONS WHY THAT COULD HAPPEN.

[POINT TO FIRST ROW ON SLIDE]

THE MODEL ALREADY HAS THE INFORMATION, BUT IT MESSES UP THE LOGIC OR THE OUTPUT FORMAT.

THIS IS A ***KNOWLEDGE GAP***, AND THE FIX IS ***RAG.***

[POINT TO THIRD ROW]

***PROBLEM THREE: "IT WON'T STAY ON-BRAND."***
THE MODEL GIVES CORRECT ANSWERS, BUT IN THE WRONG TONE, OR IN THE WRONG FORMAT, EVERY SINGLE TIME.
THIS IS A ***STYLE GAP***, AND THE FIX IS ***FINE-TUNING.***

## SLIDE 4 — MENTAL MODEL [~1 MINUTE 30 SECONDS]

[CLICK TO NEXT SLIDE]

ALRIGHT, LET ME GIVE YOU THE SIMPLEST WAY TO REMEMBER THESE THREE TECHNIQUES.

[POINT TO PROMPTING SECTION]

***PROMPTING*** IS THE ***INSTRUCTION LAYER*** —
IT'S "HOW YOU TALK TO THE MODEL."
YOU JUST GIVE IT BETTER INSTRUCTIONS.
THE EFFORT IS LOW, AND NOTHING CHANGES IN THE MODEL ITSELF.
TECHNIQUES HERE INCLUDE ZERO-SHOT, FEW-SHOT, AND CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT.

[POINT TO RAG SECTION]

***RAG*** IS THE ***KNOWLEDGE LAYER*** —
IT'S "WHAT YOU SHOW THE MODEL."
YOU FEED IT YOUR OWN DATA — YOUR DOCUMENTS, YOUR DATABASES — AT THE TIME IT ANSWERS A QUESTION.
THE EFFORT IS MEDIUM, AND WHAT CHANGES IS THE KNOWLEDGE THE MODEL CAN ACCESS.
IT INVOLVES EMBEDDINGS, VECTOR STORES, AND RETRIEVAL.

[POINT TO FINE-TUNING SECTION]

***FINE-TUNING*** IS THE ***BEHAVIOR LAYER*** —
IT'S "HOW YOU TRAIN THE MODEL."
YOU ACTUALLY CHANGE THE MODEL'S INTERNAL BRAIN SO IT BEHAVES DIFFERENTLY BY DEFAULT.
THE EFFORT IS HIGH, AND WHAT CHANGES ARE THE MODEL'S INTERNAL WEIGHTS.
TECHNIQUES INCLUDE LORA, QLORA, AND SFT.

[PAUSE. SMILE.]

NOW, HERE'S A SIMPLE ANALOGY THAT MAKES THIS CRYSTAL CLEAR —
***THE CHEF ANALOGY:***

[GESTURE WITH HANDS]

***PROMPTING*** IS LIKE GIVING THE CHEF VERBAL INSTRUCTIONS —
"MAKE IT SPICY, USE BULLET POINTS."
*SAME CHEF, SAME KNOWLEDGE — YOU'RE JUST TELLING THEM WHAT TO DO.*

***RAG*** IS LIKE HANDING THE CHEF A RECIPE BOOK —
"HERE'S OUR DATA, USE IT."
*YOU'RE GIVING THE CHEF NEW INFORMATION RIGHT WHEN THEY'RE COOKING.*

***FINE-TUNING*** IS LIKE SENDING THE CHEF TO CULINARY SCHOOL.
*THE CHEF ITSELF CHANGES — IT COMES BACK WITH NEW SKILLS BAKED IN.*

## SLIDE 5 — LAYER 1: PROMPTING [~1 MINUTE 30 SECONDS]

[CLICK TO NEXT SLIDE]

LET'S GO DEEPER INTO EACH ONE, STARTING WITH PROMPTING.

AS GOOGLE'S WHITEPAPER PUTS IT:
*"PROMPT ENGINEERING IS THE ART OF COMMUNICATING WITH A GENERATIVE AI MODEL."*

THERE ARE FIVE MAIN PROMPTING TECHNIQUES, AND THEY'RE SHOWN IN THIS TABLE:

[POINT TO TABLE ROW BY ROW]

***ZERO-SHOT*** — YOU JUST ASK THE MODEL DIRECTLY.
LIKE: "CLASSIFY THIS EMAIL AS SPAM OR NOT."
NO EXAMPLES, NO HINTS.

***FEW-SHOT*** — YOU SHOW THE MODEL A FEW EXAMPLES FIRST, THEN ASK IT TO FOLLOW THE SAME PATTERN.
THIS WORKS REALLY WELL WHEN YOU NEED THE MODEL TO FOLLOW A SPECIFIC FORMAT.

***CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT*** — YOU TELL THE MODEL TO "THINK STEP BY STEP."
THIS IS GREAT FOR MATH, LOGIC, AND ANY MULTI-STEP REASONING.

***SYSTEM PROMPT*** — YOU SET THE MODEL'S OVERALL PERSONALITY.
LIKE: "YOU ARE A JLL PROPERTY ANALYST."

***REACT*** — THIS IS FOR MORE ADVANCED AGENTIC TASKS:
THINK, ACT, OBSERVE, AND REPEAT.

[POINT TO PROS]

THE PROS OF PROMPTING?
ZERO INFRASTRUCTURE COST, INSTANT ITERATION, YOU DON'T NEED ANY TRAINING DATA, AND IT WORKS WITH ANY MODEL.

[POINT TO CONS]

THE CONS?
THE OUTPUTS CAN BE FRAGILE — SMALL WORDING CHANGES CAN COMPLETELY CHANGE THE RESULT.
YOU CAN'T INJECT PRIVATE COMPANY DATA.
AND AT SCALE, THE OUTPUTS MAY NOT BE CONSISTENT.

[POINT TO CODE EXAMPLE]

ON THE RIGHT, YOU CAN SEE A PYTHON CODE EXAMPLE OF ***FEW-SHOT PROMPTING.***
THE KEY THING TO NOTICE IS THAT WE'RE GIVING THE MODEL TWO EXAMPLES FIRST —
"RENT FAILED" MAPS TO BILLING, "ELEVATOR STUCK" MAPS TO MAINTENANCE —
AND THEN WE ASK IT TO CLASSIFY "PARKING SENSOR DOWN."
AND IT CORRECTLY OUTPUTS MAINTENANCE AT 96% CONFIDENCE.
THE EXAMPLES ***LOCK THE OUTPUT FORMAT***, WHICH IS EXACTLY WHAT WE NEED WHEN OTHER SYSTEMS DEPEND ON PARSING THAT OUTPUT.

[PAUSE]

***THE BOTTOM LINE: IF PROMPTING IS FRAGILE FOR YOUR USE CASE — THAT'S YOUR SIGNAL TO MOVE TO THE NEXT LEVEL.***

## SLIDE 6 — LAYER 2: RAG - HOW IT WORKS [~1 MINUTE 30 SECONDS]

[CLICK TO NEXT SLIDE]

NOW, RAG — WHICH STANDS FOR RETRIEVAL-AUGMENTED GENERATION.
***THIS IS THE MOST COMMON PATTERN WE SEE IN ENTERPRISE AI.***

AS GOOGLE'S WHITEPAPER SAYS:
*"RAG CONNECTS LLMS TO EXTERNAL DATA, PROVIDING FACTUAL GROUNDING WITHOUT RETRAINING."*

IN SIMPLE WORDS — IF THE MODEL NEEDS TO KNOW THINGS IT WASN'T TRAINED ON —
YOUR COMPANY DATA, LEASE DOCUMENTS, POLICIES —
RAG IS THE ANSWER.
AND YOU DON'T NEED TO RETRAIN THE MODEL AT ALL.

RAG HAS TWO PHASES:

[POINT TO LEFT SIDE — INGESTION]

***PHASE 1 — INGESTION.***
THIS IS DONE OFFLINE, JUST ONCE.
YOU TAKE YOUR DOCUMENTS — PDFS, LEASES, POLICIES —
AND YOU BREAK THEM INTO SMALL CHUNKS.
THEN YOU CONVERT THOSE CHUNKS INTO NUMBERS CALLED EMBEDDINGS USING AN EMBEDDING MODEL.
AND FINALLY, YOU STORE THOSE EMBEDDINGS IN A VECTOR STORE —
*THINK OF IT LIKE A SMART DATABASE THAT UNDERSTANDS MEANING, NOT JUST KEYWORDS.*

[POINT TO RIGHT SIDE — RETRIEVAL]

***PHASE 2 — RETRIEVAL.***
THIS HAPPENS AT RUNTIME, EVERY TIME A USER ASKS A QUESTION.
THE USER'S QUESTION IS ALSO CONVERTED INTO AN EMBEDDING.
THEN WE DO A SIMILARITY SEARCH IN THE VECTOR STORE —
BASICALLY ASKING, "WHICH CHUNKS OF OUR DOCUMENTS ARE MOST SIMILAR TO THIS QUESTION?"
WE PICK THE TOP FEW RESULTS, RERANK THEM FOR QUALITY,
AND THEN SEND THEM TO THE AI MODEL ALONG WITH THE QUESTION.
THE MODEL NOW ANSWERS BASED ON YOUR ACTUAL DOCUMENTS — ***NOT FROM ITS IMAGINATION.***

[PAUSE]

TWO THINGS THAT MAKE OR BREAK RAG:
***CHUNKING STRATEGY*** — BAD CHUNKING EQUALS BAD ANSWERS.
AND ALWAYS ***RERANKING*** IN PRODUCTION —
SENDING THE TOP 3 TO 5 RERANKED CHUNKS IS MUCH BETTER THAN SENDING 20 RANDOM ONES.

## SLIDE 8 — LAYER 3: FINE-TUNING [~1 MINUTE 30 SECONDS]

[CLICK TO NEXT SLIDE]

ALRIGHT, NOW FINE-TUNING —

[PAUSE FOR EFFECT]

AND I WANT TO SAY THIS STRONGLY:
***FINE-TUNING IS THE LAST RESORT, NOT THE FIRST.***

AS GOOGLE'S WHITEPAPER SAYS:
*"FINE-TUNING UPDATES A MODEL'S INTERNAL WEIGHTS USING DOMAIN DATA TO CHANGE ITS DEFAULT BEHAVIOR."*

ITS REAL VALUE IS ***BEHAVIORAL CONSISTENCY*** —
IT MAKES THE MODEL ALWAYS USE A SPECIFIC FORMAT, A SPECIFIC TONE, A SPECIFIC SCHEMA —
*WITHOUT YOU HAVING TO REMIND IT EVERY TIME IN THE PROMPT.*

THE PROCESS HAS FOUR STEPS:

[POINT TO EACH STEP]

***STEP 1 — CURATE TRAINING DATA.***
YOU CREATE A JSONL FILE WITH 50 TO 500 OR MORE HIGH-QUALITY EXAMPLES.

***STEP 2 — CLEAN AND VALIDATE.***
REMOVE NOISE, MAKE SURE THE EXAMPLES ARE CONSISTENT.

***STEP 3 — TRAIN.***
YOU USE METHODS LIKE LORA OR QLORA TO FINE-TUNE THE BASE MODEL.
LORA IS THE MOST PRACTICAL APPROACH.
QLORA ADDS A TECHNIQUE CALLED QUANTIZATION, WHICH LETS YOU TRAIN ON SMALLER, LESS EXPENSIVE HARDWARE.

***STEP 4 — EVALUATE AND DEPLOY.***
CHECK VALIDATION LOSS, RUN TASK-SPECIFIC METRICS, AND DO A/B TESTING.

[POINT TO JSONL EXAMPLE]

THE TRAINING DATA FORMAT IS SHOWN HERE —
EACH EXAMPLE IS A CONVERSATION WITH A SYSTEM MESSAGE, A USER MESSAGE, AND THE IDEAL ASSISTANT RESPONSE,
ALL IN ONE LINE OF JSON.

[POINT TO PYTHON CODE]

FOR CLAUDE, FINE-TUNING RUNS THROUGH AMAZON BEDROCK.
THE CODE IS JUST A FEW LINES USING BOTO3.

[PAUSE. LEAN IN SLIGHTLY.]

NOW, HERE'S WHAT PEOPLE GET WRONG ABOUT THE COST OF FINE-TUNING.
THE BIGGEST COST IS ***NOT*** THE CLOUD COMPUTE BILL.
***THE BIGGEST COST IS YOUR TEAM'S TIME*** —
ANYWHERE FROM 40 TO 200 PLUS HOURS —
JUST TO CURATE HIGH-QUALITY TRAINING DATA.
*THAT'S WEEKS TO MONTHS OF WORK.*

## SLIDE 13 — COMMON MISTAKES [~1 MINUTE 30 SECONDS]

[CLICK TO NEXT SLIDE]

***THIS SLIDE IS REALLY IMPORTANT*** — THESE ARE MISTAKES FROM ACTUAL PROJECTS.

[POINT TO PROMPTING SECTION]

***PROMPTING MISTAKES:***

VAGUE INSTRUCTIONS — "SUMMARIZE THIS" DOESN'T WORK WELL.
INSTEAD, SAY "3 BULLETS, MAX 20 WORDS EACH, FOCUS ON FINANCIAL IMPACT."

NO OUTPUT FORMAT — DON'T HOPE THE MODEL GUESSES.
***SPECIFY A JSON SCHEMA.***

NO NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS — ALWAYS ADD THINGS LIKE
***"DO NOT INVENT INFORMATION. SAY 'I DON'T KNOW' IF THE DATA ISN'T THERE."***

[POINT TO RAG SECTION]

***RAG MISTAKES:***

FIXED-SIZE CHUNKING — BLINDLY CUTTING AT 512 TOKENS DESTROYS TABLES AND CONTEXT.
USE SEMANTIC CHUNKING INSTEAD.

VECTOR-ONLY SEARCH — THIS CAN MISS EXACT IDS AND CODES.
USE HYBRID SEARCH THAT COMBINES VECTOR AND KEYWORD SEARCH.

CONTEXT STUFFING — SHOVING 20 PLUS CHUNKS INTO THE PROMPT CAUSES A PROBLEM CALLED "LOST IN THE MIDDLE,"
WHERE THE MODEL IGNORES THE MIDDLE CHUNKS.
***SEND ONLY THE TOP 3 TO 5 RERANKED CHUNKS.***

[POINT TO FINE-TUNING SECTION. SLOW DOWN.]

***FINE-TUNING MISTAKES, AND THIS IS THE BIG ONE:***

***THE NUMBER ONE MISTAKE*** IN THE INDUSTRY IS USING FINE-TUNING TO TEACH THE MODEL FACTS.

PICTURE THIS:
A MANAGER SAYS "THE MODEL DOESN'T KNOW OUR LEASE TERMS,"
AND THE DEVELOPER SAYS "LET'S FINE-TUNE IT ON OUR DOCUMENTS!"

*THAT SOUNDS LOGICAL, BUT IT'S WRONG.*

***THE RIGHT ANSWER:***
THIS IS A ***KNOWLEDGE*** PROBLEM → USE ***RAG.***

***FINE-TUNING TEACHES STYLE, NOT CONTENT.***
***IT TEACHES THE MODEL HOW TO RESPOND, NOT WHAT TO SAY.***

ALSO — ALWAYS TRY PROMPT PLUS RAG BEFORE JUMPING TO FINE-TUNING.
AND YOU NEED AT LEAST 50 TO 500 QUALITY EXAMPLES.
*LESS THAN 20 WON'T WORK.*
